# UmaTeam6

MiniWorld Description:
The theme park is separated into multiple themed zones, each of which has a variety of attractions, dining options, retail stores, tourist services, parking, hotels, and designated employees. Visitors purchase General, VIP, or Membership tickets, spend the day on rides and other attractions, and have the option to post reviews once they've been.
Behind the scenes, staff members manage maintenance, assist with restaurants and retail, handle attractions, and assist visitors. These teams are supervised by managers, who also approve maintenance, keep an eye on inventory, and produce operational reports. The system's configurations, security, user access, and general database management are all entirely under the administrators' control.    
Retail stores sell goods from approved vendors, and managers keep an eye on the inventory, which is managed by staff. Restrooms, guest services, lockers, and hotels are examples of visitor amenities; hotel records record information such as room capacity, star rating, and contact details. In order to help operational planning, safety choices, and ride availability, the system also records daily weather conditions and associates them with attractions.    

Function Requirements:
-Manage attraction details and operational status
-Record dining facility information
-Track shop inventory and pricing
-Manage employee and manager records
-Store visitor, ticket information, visitor reviews, and feedback
-Track maintenance activities and record accident history
-Manage facilities, including hotels, and manage parking information
-Organize the park structure by themed areas
-Track weather conditions and operational impact
-Generate reports for management and operational planning
-Must be able to modify and delete existing data and add new data
-Recalculate ride and show wait times every 30 min.
-Admin will be able to access financial records, employee data, and visitor data

Overview:
The conceptual schema follows an Entity-Relationship (ER) model with Area as the central entity. Attractions, restaurants, shops, facilities, hotels, employees, and parking are directly related to areas. Visitors interact with tickets and reviews. Attractions interact with maintenance, accident history, and weather records. Suppliers provide items to shops, and managers supervise employees. Weather conditions influence attraction operations.
